#### 1. Malaysia's e-waste import ban confronts smuggling and enforcement gaps

- **Source:** [Mongabay](https://news.mongabay.com/2026/07/malaysia-clamps-down-on-toxic-trade-with-total-e-waste-import-ban/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** Effective enforcement could reduce hazardous pollution while giving compliant domestic recyclers a fairer market.

Malaysia began enforcing an absolute e-waste import ban on April 1, but Mongabay reports that smugglers continue to disguise hazardous cargo as industrial commodities. The Op Green Shield task force inspected 685 containers between January 26 and July 7 and returned 143 containers carrying 3,057 metric tons of e-waste; another 531 containers, including an earlier backlog, were designated for phased repatriation beginning July 8. Officials say the enormous volume moving through Malaysian ports makes detection difficult, while residents near illegal processing sites continue to report suspected waste burning.

#### 2. Bank Negara plans measured foreign-exchange liberalisation

- **Source:** [The Star, reporting Bernama](https://www.thestar.com.my/business/business-news/2026/07/21/bank-negara-to-continue-promoting-two-way-flows-in-onshore-financial-market)
- **Published:** 2026-07-21
- **Why it matters:** Further calibrated liberalisation could deepen Malaysia's onshore market while preserving safeguards against volatile capital flows and external-debt risk.

Bank Negara Malaysia Governor Abdul Rasheed Ghaffour said the central bank will continue promoting two-way flows through gradual changes to its foreign-exchange policy framework. He said average daily onshore foreign-exchange turnover reached US$21.4 billion as of June 2026, up from US$19.8 billion in 2025, and highlighted the Qualified Resident Investor programme as a way for eligible companies to redeploy repatriated funds abroad without seeking approval for every transaction. Future changes will be informed by engagement with businesses and market participants.

#### 3. Pos Malaysia cuts quarterly loss as government studies postal-service fund

- **Source:** [Malay Mail, reporting Bernama](https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2026/07/21/pos-malaysia-focused-on-cutting-losses-through-transformation-plan-says-ministry/228407)
- **Published:** 2026-07-21
- **Why it matters:** A sustainable funding mechanism is central to maintaining universal postal service in commercially unviable areas without relying on recurring direct bailouts.

The Communications Ministry said Pos Malaysia reduced its net loss to RM19.5 million in the first quarter of 2026 from RM41.5 million a year earlier, its lowest quarterly loss in four years. The improvement was attributed to digital certificate and printing revenue, stronger aviation performance, and smaller postal-service losses. Rather than direct financial assistance, the government is examining a Postal Service Fund to support universal service in less commercially viable areas.

#### 1. Judge gives final approval to Anthropic's US$1.5 billion copyright settlement

- **Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/20/anthropics-landmark-1-5b-copyright-settlement-is-approved/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** The record-scale payout resolves Anthropic's piracy exposure in this case but leaves the broader legality of training AI on copyrighted works unsettled.

A US federal judge gave final approval to Anthropic's US$1.5 billion settlement with authors and publishers, covering an estimated 500,000 works at US$3,000 per work. An earlier ruling found that model training on copyrighted text could qualify as fair use, but that Anthropic's downloading and storage of books from pirate sites was illegal. Because the case settled without appellate review, that district-court ruling does not bind other courts hearing AI copyright cases.

- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** Simpler, more scalable MCP infrastructure could accelerate reliable enterprise deployment of AI agents that connect to external tools and data.

A forthcoming Model Context Protocol update will loosen its server-side reliance on session IDs and move toward a more stateless approach. TechCrunch reports that the existing design creates extra coordination work when requests are distributed across many servers and regions behind load balancers. The change is intended to make MCP services easier and potentially cheaper to operate at scale, although the technical update will largely be invisible to end users.

### Malaysia News

#### 1. Dewan Negara passes Cybercrimes Bill to replace 1997 law

- **Source:** [Fintech News Malaysia](https://fintechnews.my/59708/security/malaysia-cybercrimes-bill/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-21
- **Why it matters:** Replacing the nearly three-decade-old statute would broaden Malaysia's legal toolkit against technology-enabled and cross-border crime while retaining stated protections for lawful expression.

The Dewan Negara approved the Cybercrimes Bill 2026, which would repeal the Computer Crimes Act 1997 and create a broader framework for online offences, including crimes committed using artificial intelligence. The government said lawful journalism, academic work and freedom of expression would not be targeted, and that offences carrying possible prison terms of at least three years could support extradition. Senators also urged tougher action against large fraud syndicates, victim compensation, remedies for compromised digital identities, and stronger authentication than SMS one-time passwords.

#### 2. ECRL projected to add up to RM90 billion to GDP by 2047

- **Source:** [The Edge Malaysia, reporting Bernama](https://theedgemalaysia.com/node/811491)
- **Published:** 2026-07-21
- **Why it matters:** The projection quantifies the government's long-term economic case for the RM50.27 billion rail project beyond its immediate transport benefits.

Deputy Economy Minister Mohd Shahar Abdullah said the East Coast Rail Link is projected to generate RM80 billion to RM90 billion in cumulative value added to Malaysia's gross domestic product by 2047. He attributed the expected gains to 21 economic accelerator projects, including planned logistics hubs in Pasir Puteh, Kemaman and Temerloh. The 665km ECRL is scheduled for completion in December 2026 and operations in January 2027.

### AI News

#### 1. Malaysia plans risk-based AI Governance Bill after consultations

- **Source:** [The Vibes](https://www.thevibes.com/articles/news/125269/ai-governance-bill-to-be-tabled-in-parliament-after-ministry-agency-consultations)
- **Published:** 2026-07-21
- **Why it matters:** A lifecycle-wide, risk-based law could establish binding responsibilities for organizations that build, provide, operate or use AI in Malaysia.

Communications Minister Fahmi Fadzil said a dedicated AI Governance Bill will be tabled in the Dewan Rakyat after ministry, agency and Cabinet consultations are complete. The proposed framework would apply across the AI lifecycle and require governance measures proportionate to risk, while the government says it does not intend to deter innovation or investment. Fahmi added that regulators will continue using existing laws in the interim and cited guardrails added after the Malaysian Communications and Multimedia Commission contacted X about inappropriate images generated by Grok.
