### Malaysia News

#### 1. Malaysia's first-half trade reaches record RM1.796 trillion

- **Source:** [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/business/business-news/2026/07/20/malaysia039s-trade-surges-224-to-record-rm1796-trillion-in-1h26)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** Record trade and export values show strong external demand, particularly for Malaysia's electrical and electronics sector, despite global uncertainty.

Malaysia's trade rose 22.4% year on year to RM1.796 trillion in the first half of 2026, according to figures from the Ministry of Investment, Trade and Industry reported by The Star. Exports increased 27.5% to RM971.59 billion, imports rose 16.9% to RM824.44 billion, and the trade surplus reached RM147.15 billion; electrical and electronic products accounted for nearly RM140 billion of the increase in export value. June alone recorded RM340.89 billion in total trade and marked the 74th consecutive monthly surplus.

#### 2. MARC Ratings raises Malaysia's 2026 growth forecast to 5.1%

- **Source:** [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/business/business-news/2026/07/20/marc-ratings-raises-malaysia039s-2026-gdp-growth-forecast-to-51)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** The upgrade signals stronger-than-expected momentum, while highlighting inflation and interest-rate risks from geopolitical tensions.

MARC Ratings upgraded its forecast for Malaysia's 2026 gross domestic product growth to 5.1% from 4.4%, with a possible upward bias if third-quarter momentum remains strong. It cited supply-chain investment, infrastructure development, tourism, hydrocarbon exports, foreign direct investment, the semiconductor and AI investment cycle, and resilient private consumption. The agency expects the overnight policy rate to remain unchanged in its baseline case but said elevated oil prices could add inflation pressure.

#### 3. Anwar defends fuel policy against calls for deeper price cuts

- **Source:** [The Star](https://www.thestar.com.my/news/nation/2026/07/20/lowering-fuel-prices-would-only-make-national-debt-pile-up-says-anwar)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** Fuel subsidies are a major fiscal-policy trade-off between household costs and containing public debt.

Prime Minister and Finance Minister Anwar Ibrahim said the government already spends RM40 billion annually on petrol and diesel subsidies and cannot fund deeper price cuts by allowing debt to keep rising. He said Malaysia imports about half of its petroleum needs while subsidised RON95 petrol is sold at RM1.95 per litre. The remarks came in response to social-media calls to reduce petrol to RM1.50 per litre.

### AI News

#### 1. Current AI advances open, public-interest AI infrastructure

- **Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/19/nonprofit-current-ai-is-racing-to-build-the-world-wide-web-of-ai-free-for-all/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-19
- **Why it matters:** Public-interest infrastructure could broaden access to AI while giving underrepresented language communities more control over their data and models.

Nonprofit Current AI is building open AI infrastructure, including an open-source offline device developed with India's Bhashini that supports 22 Indian languages. TechCrunch reports that the organization has $400 million in committed funding and recently allocated $3.2 million to projects involving African languages, Arab cultural data, Indigenous Amazon communities, and AI accountability. Its stated aim is to offer a public alternative to privately controlled AI systems, with models and data managed locally where possible.

#### 2. Netflix discloses $587 million price for AI filmmaking startup

- **Source:** [TechCrunch](https://techcrunch.com/2026/07/19/netflix-paid-587m-for-ben-afflecks-ai-filmmaking-startup/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-19
- **Why it matters:** The disclosed price demonstrates how strategically valuable generative-AI production tools have become to major entertainment platforms.

Netflix disclosed in a regulatory filing that it paid $587 million in cash for InterPositive, an AI filmmaking startup co-founded by Ben Affleck, after announcing the acquisition in March without financial terms. Its tools are intended to address post-production problems such as missing shots, background replacements, and incorrect lighting. Netflix also said around 300 of its titles have already used generative AI.

#### 3. LG AI Research and UNESCO partner launch global AI ethics course

- **Source:** [The Korea Herald](https://www.koreaherald.com/article/10813917)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** A globally available, practitioner-focused course can help translate high-level AI ethics principles into development and deployment practices.

LG AI Research and the Korean National Commission for UNESCO launched a 10-module AI ethics massive open online course for developers, researchers, and policymakers worldwide. Built with Coursera, the curriculum involved experts from institutions including Carnegie Mellon, Stanford, the Oxford Internet Institute, and the Alan Turing Institute, plus an advisory group representing 15 organizations. The partners plan a Korean pilot for AI practitioners later in 2026 and expansion to five to 10 regional universities in 2027.

#### 2. AI-altered bird images threaten citizen-science records

- **Source:** [The Guardian](https://www.theguardian.com/environment/2026/jul/20/ai-slop-manipulated-fake-images-birds-citizen-science-aoe)
- **Published:** 2026-07-20
- **Why it matters:** Contaminated observation databases could undermine ecological research that relies on large volumes of public-submitted evidence.
