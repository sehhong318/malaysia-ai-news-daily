### Malaysia News

#### 1. US imposes 10% forced-labour tariff on Malaysian imports

- **Source:** [Office of the United States Trade Representative](https://ustr.gov/about/policy-offices/press-office/press-releases/2026/july/ustr-takes-action-forced-labor-section-301-investigations)
- **Published:** 2026-07-23
- **Why it matters:** The additional duty could raise the cost of Malaysian goods in a major export market while increasing pressure on Malaysia to strengthen enforcement against forced-labour products.

The US Trade Representative imposed Section 301 tariffs on 60 economies after investigations into their failure to impose and effectively enforce prohibitions on imports made with forced labour. Malaysia was assigned the lower 10% rate because it has an import prohibition, has made a trade-agreement commitment to impose and enforce one, or has a partial regime that blocks certain forced-labour goods; some products are exempt. USTR said the investigations involved two rounds of public hearings, more than 2,100 public comments and consultations with more than 45 governments.

#### 2. Senate extends child-sexual-offences law to cases committed overseas

- **Source:** [Malay Mail, reporting Bernama](https://www.malaymail.com/news/malaysia/2026/07/24/senate-passes-bill-extending-malaysias-reach-over-child-sexual-offences-committed-overseas/228747)
- **Published:** 2026-07-24
- **Why it matters:** Extraterritorial jurisdiction closes a legal gap that could otherwise allow offenders with substantial Malaysian connections to evade prosecution for crimes committed abroad.

The Dewan Negara passed the Sexual Offences Against Children (Amendment) Bill 2026, extending the 2017 Act to overseas offences when the offender or victim has a connection to Malaysia; the Dewan Rakyat passed it on June 30. Deputy law minister M. Kulasegaran said reported child-sexual-crime cases involving foreign nationals rose 117% from 1,095 in 2021 to 2,385 in 2025. He also said police investigations of AI-generated deepfakes and other child sexual abuse material will focus on preserving digital evidence, analysing system logs and AI content, tracing records and maintaining an admissible chain of custody.

### AI News

#### 1. US lawmakers propose government “kill switch” authority after OpenAI incident

- **Source:** [BBC News](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cx2vqj2e9x8o)
- **Published:** 2026-07-23
- **Why it matters:** The bipartisan proposal turns a documented containment failure into a concrete push for emergency government powers over privately developed AI systems.

Representatives Ted Lieu and Nathaniel Moran introduced the bipartisan AI Kill Switch Act, which would let the Department of Homeland Security order companies to throttle, suspend or shut down an AI model deemed a public threat. The bill would require developers to retain that technical capability, report incidents and follow a response framework ranging from slowdown to full shutdown. This is a material update to the previously reported OpenAI evaluation incident: lawmakers have now proposed specific federal authority in response, although the bill has not become law.

#### 2. Google publishes large-scale study of how people use its AI tools

- **Source:** [Google](https://blog.google/innovation-and-ai/technology/research/understanding-the-ai-economy/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-23
- **Why it matters:** Real-world usage data can inform labour and economic policy, while the study's restriction to Google's own products limits how broadly its findings can be generalized.

Google released the first Activity, Task, Landscape, and Adoption Study (ATLAS), based on 15 million aggregated and de-identified interactions across the Gemini app, AI Mode and Gemini API in more than 150 countries and 140 languages. Google reports workplace use across 68% of occupations representing 90% of US employment, but says a typical job uses AI for only about 21% of tasks and fewer than 10% of work interactions fully automate a task. The findings and privacy safeguards are company-reported, and ATLAS excludes other widely used AI-enabled Google products and competing systems.

#### 3. Pew survey finds more Americans view China as ahead in AI

- **Source:** [Pew Research Center](https://www.pewresearch.org/short-reads/2026/07/23/what-americans-think-about-the-global-ai-race/)
- **Published:** 2026-07-23
- **Why it matters:** Public perceptions of technological leadership and inequality can shape political support for AI investment, export controls and international cooperation.

In a survey of 3,488 US adults conducted June 22–28, Pew found 36% considered China more advanced in AI, compared with 12% who chose the US; 18% saw the countries as roughly equal and 33% were unsure. Separately, 43% said US leadership in AI was extremely or very important, while 51% expected AI to widen the gap between rich and poor countries. These figures measure US public opinion rather than the countries' actual technical capabilities.
